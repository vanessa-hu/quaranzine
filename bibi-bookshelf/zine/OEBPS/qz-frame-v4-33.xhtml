33

Or did they blame each other,

As my list of reasons to put off reading

grew longer than my hair?

How did they prove themselves?

Did Nietzche gloat about all the passages I underlined

as Eragon unfurled his worn, ripped corners?

Did the old-timers muster to oust the invaders?

Did the Rohirrim trot across my dusty desk,

with the Nautilus ferrying the V.F.D. over my treacherous blue rug?

Did the other army bring tactics that had never been seen in my room?

Waka, free indirect style, supply-and-demand curves.

As I washed my hands, did Du Bois, Arendt, and Foucault draw up battle plans?

As I hit the last “submit” of the semester, did the newcomers fling a stinging, necessary rejoinder:

That the world is deeper, older, and not nearly as white as the old books believed.

Nor, they whispered, was I.

Did Achilles rest easy in Huck’s tent?

As I rearranged my shelves, did the conquerors sense why I had so much space for books?

And so much time, even when I did not take it.

As I squabbled with my parents, did they manage forgiveness?

As I ignored them to stare forward at the same headlines and charts,

Did Lily Briscoe, Úrsula Buendía, Spock, or Ford Prefect figure out something about time?

As I stayed in my room, did they become one collection?

Did they make sense in any order?

Did they consider working together to rescue Harry Potter from my sister’s room across the hall?
