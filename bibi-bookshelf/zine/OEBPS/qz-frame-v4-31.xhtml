31

One of the odder parts of coming home suddenly,

without summer storage,

is seeing all my books in one place.

I wonder if the characters spring off shelves and slide along walls.

You know, Toy Story-style;

I wonder if they get along.

Did my older books welcome their new neighbors?

As I unpacked my suitcases,

did Merlin and Gandalf watch warily?

Did they meet before the dorm-smell faded?

Did Shahrazad, bolder than the rest,

find Scout Finch peering behind a paperweight?

As I downloaded Zoom, did they connect?

Could “Raoul et ses amis” from Discovering French, Volume One

even understand what Camus was on about?

As I wore sweatpants around the house

did Tiresias trust Tris, who insisted that I always dressed that way?

Did Mr. Benedict believe Dr. Frankenstein, who claimed I had a new look?

As another FaceTime ended past midnight,

did Westley and Hazel scoff at Sappho and Mr. Knightley’s commentary,

But, privately, feel a little insecure?

As I paced at 2:00 AM

without going to them,

did Socrates and the Boxcar Children worry together?

Just a Position

by Michael Baick
