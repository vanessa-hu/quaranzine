EYES

Anonymously Submitted

I

I was probably in Kindergarten when I learned what double eyelids were. My family was sitting down for lunch at our dimly lit kitchen table on a Saturday before piano lessons. “You’re lucky you got my good genes!” My mother ran her finger along the crease in her eyelid, totally serious but almost smirking. “This line above your eye – if you have it, it’s called double eyes. We all have it.” She leaned in, glancing quickly toward our neighbor’s house and lowering her voice as her smile widened. “Eh, the girls in my high school would have killed for these eyes. Killed! And our eyes are big, too. This is the ideal type, you know.” Her eyes got huge and she started to whisper. “All my friends were Japanese girls, too, and some of them even taped their eyes so they would come like mine.” I nodded even though I didn’t really understand. Were our eyes that big? What line was she talking about? I couldn’t see anything on her face, but it seemed important.

“Too bad, though, you got my nose, too. Real flat kind. I don’t like it.”

II

My parents didn’t let us watch a lot of TV when we were young, so we spent basically all of our time reading. The Harry Potter books were the first I deeply loved: Finally, books that were limitless, with enough characters and magic and lore and pages to feel exciting each time I reread them. For about a year, I always had a copy of one of the books on me and plopped down to read them whenever I could. I had messy hair and ovular brown glasses, so sometimes people would see me with one of the books and tell me I looked like Harry.
