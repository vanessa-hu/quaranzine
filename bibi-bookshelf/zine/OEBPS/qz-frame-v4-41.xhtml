VI

I asked my sister what she thought about eyes after explaining the premise of this essay to her. She didn’t think about eyes at all, she informed me. Eyelashes and eyebrows, though, were a big deal. I hadn’t really noticed what she had done to her own eyebrows until she mentioned this, but they were meticulously shaped. I was shocked – obviously our expe-riences weren’t identical, but how could she not think about this? It seemed increasingly likely to me that nothing I wrote was relatable and this whole thing was somehow even more self-indulgent than I had initially thought. I mean, there’s people dying, Kim! People are being deported and assaulted and devastated by climate change and the honeybees are disappearing and health care is unaffordable and I spent several hours writing a non-an-alytical, non-revolutionary piece of whiny drivel about how my eyes make me kind of privileged, kind of not, and very self-conscious.

The kicker is, after all of this, I’m still really anxious about my eyes. I don’t think they look nice and I feel really guilty for feeling that way. But even if this whole essay was a waste of your time, and even if this means nothing to you, as it likely does not, I can say that I think your eyes are beautiful! And I hope you see it that way, too.
